## GTM Motion Types

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- **Best when:** Product has self-serve value, low barrier to try
- **Pikar-AI fit:** Medium — needs some setup, but demo/sandbox could work
- **Tactics:** Free tier, interactive demo, product tours, in-app virality

### Sales-Led Growth (SLG)
- **Best when:** High ACV, complex buying process, enterprise
- **Pikar-AI fit:** Future state for enterprise tier
- **Tactics:** Demo calls, POCs, case studies, white-glove onboarding

### Community-Led Growth (CLG)
- **Best when:** Passionate user base, knowledge-sharing culture
- **Pikar-AI fit:** High — founders love sharing tools and workflows
- **Tactics:** Community, content, events, user-generated content

### Recommended for Pikar-AI: **PLG + CLG Hybrid**
- Free/trial tier drives initial adoption
- Community creates stickiness and word-of-mouth
- Upgrade path to paid tiers based on agent usage or features

## Launch Sequencing

### The Three-Wave Launch

**Wave 1: Seed (T-60 to T-30)**
- Build waiting list
- Seed community with 20-50 founding members
- Publish foundational content (3-5 blog posts, social presence)
- Soft press outreach to build relationships

**Wave 2: Build (T-30 to T-0)**
- Ramp content and social posting
- Beta test with community members
- Secure press embargoes
- Prepare all launch day assets
- Product Hunt preparation

**Wave 3: Launch (T-0 to T+30)**
- Simultaneous: press release, Product Hunt, social blitz, email blast
- Daily content cadence for first 2 weeks
- Founder visible everywhere (AMAs, comments, DMs)
- Rapid response to feedback

### Post-Launch Sustain (T+30 to T+90)
- Shift from launch hype to steady growth content
- Case studies from early users
- SEO content starts ranking
- Community self-sustaining with ambassador program
- First paid experiments based on organic data

## PMF Assessment Framework

### Sean Ellis Test
Ask early users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use Pikar-AI?"
- Very disappointed → PMF signal
- Somewhat disappointed → Getting closer
- Not disappointed → Problem

**Target:** 40%+ "very disappointed" = PMF

### PMF Indicators for Pikar-AI

| Signal | Weak | Moderate | Strong |
|--------|------|----------|--------|
| Organic signups | <10/week | 10-50/week | 50+/week |
| Activation rate | <20% | 20-40% | 40%+ |
| Weekly retention | <30% | 30-50% | 50%+ |
| NPS | <20 | 20-50 | 50+ |
| Organic referrals | Rare | Some | Regular |
| Time to value | >30 min | 10-30 min | <10 min |

## Pricing Strategy Frameworks

### Value-Based Pricing for AI Products

**Framework:** Price = % of value delivered

If Pikar-AI saves a founder 20 hours/week of executive tasks, and that time is worth $150/hr:
- Value delivered: $3,000/week = $12,000/month
- Price at 10% value capture: $1,200/month
- Price at 5% value capture: $600/month

### Tier Structure Template

| Tier | Target | Agents | Price Signal |
|------|--------|--------|-------------|
| Free/Trial | Try before you buy | 2-3 agents, limited | $0 |
| Starter | Solo founders | All 10, basic | $XX/mo |
| Growth | Small teams | All 10, advanced | $XXX/mo |
| Enterprise | Large orgs | All 10, custom | Contact us |

### Launch Pricing Tactics
- **Founding member discount** — 50% lifetime for first 100 customers
- **Annual discount** — 2 months free on annual billing
- **Waiting list reward** — Top 50 on list get 3 months free

## Competitive Analysis Template

### Direct Competitors (Multi-agent AI platforms)
Analyze each for: features, pricing, positioning, target audience, strengths, weaknesses, market share estimate.

### Indirect Competitors (What people use instead)
- Virtual assistants (human)
- Single-purpose AI tools (ChatGPT, Jasper, etc.)
- Business automation platforms (Zapier, Make)
- Fractional executives (human)
- Doing it themselves (the real competition)
